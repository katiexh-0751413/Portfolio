Matching hiring needs to qualified, diverse candidates. Happy to be Included addresses the challenging task of balancing diverse hiring needs with finding the most qualified candidates. In a climate where diversity in the workplace is a priority, recruiters face the dilemma of maintaining quality while striving for inclusivity. This project aims to spot areas lacking diversity and offers practical solutions to source diverse, qualified candidates.
Our Team Poster
BACKEND ENGINEER
As the backend engineer, I managed the development of critical technical components for Happy to be Included. This involved creating and managing a robust Backend Database on Microsoft SQL Server, architecting tables such as Employees, Hiring, Candidates, Work Experience, Education, Skills, and Diversity. Our database structure empowered hiring personnel to efficiently filter applicants, specifically identifying diverse and qualified candidates.
Using Python with Flask and pyodbc, I facilitated real-time access to applicant data, ensuring seamless communication between the backend database and the frontend interface. This integration was pivotal for efficient candidate evaluation and selection.
ADDITIONAL ROLES
Beyond backend implementation, my role extended to various aspects of project management and research. Active involvement as a notetaker in interviews with recruiters and HR professionals provided vital insights into legal aspects of diversity in recruitment, varied definitions of diversity in companies, hiring practices, and recruiter needs.
I also contributed to project planning, ideation sessions, and project management tasks. This involved facilitating brainstorming sessions, structuring project timelines, and aligning team objectives throughout the project lifecycle.
SELECTED WORK
Post-project completion, I undertook the independent redesign of our UI, leveraging user research insights and project outcomes. This redesign aimed to optimize usability and better align the interface with user needs and project objectives.
