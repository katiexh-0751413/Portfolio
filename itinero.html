Seamlessly blending adventure and organization. Itinero is about balancing the thrill of exploration with the need for efficient organization.
USER RESEARCH
We conducted user research to understand the nuanced travel planning behaviors and pain points of our target audience (any travelers ages 16+), guiding the development of Itinero towards intuitive features that resonate with users' diverse needs and motivations.
I conducted semi-structured interviews with five participants and affinity diagrammed them to categorize user insights into themes.

PAPER WIREFRAMES
To initiate the design process, I sketched out wireframes on paper to visualize the core features of the app. These basic sketches laid the foundation for the app's structure, focusing on key elements such as the homepage, itinerary creation, recommendation pages, and collaborative features. The simplicity of paper wireframes allowed for rapid ideation and iteration, ensuring a clear understanding of the app's flow and functionality.
Paper Wireframes with Labels
MOOD BOARD
I wrote out on a piece of paper some words and feelings typically experienced during travel that I wanted to incorporate into my design.
Brainstormed Words on Paper
From there, I gathered some images from Pinterest to build a mood board. My goal was to capture the essence of travel, featuring things such as passports, suitcases, travel journals, cameras, maps, airplanes, and various places across the world.

I realized my mood board featured a lot of calming blue shades, so I decided to go with blue for the logo and primary accent color. This color evokes a sense of calm and resonates with the emotions associated with explorationâ€”joy, inspiration, and relaxation. Against an off-white background (#FDFDFD), the design maintains clarity and simplicity, while dark black (#333333) ensures legible typography for a seamless user experience. The curated images from Pinterest serve as visual anchors, guiding the design direction with a harmonious blend of colors that mirror the emotions and experiences tied to travel.
Mood Board
LOGO DESIGN
I started by first sketching various concepts on paper. I explored diverse ideas, incorporating travel elements such as location pins, compasses, and planes to capture the essence of the brand. Through this, I aimed to identify a concept that would effectively convey the app's core theme while maintaining simplicity and clarity.
Initial Logo Sketches on Paper
I decided I liked the minimalist concept featuring "itinero" with the location pin as the dot on the "i," so I transitioned to the high-fidelity stage. Utilizing Canva, I crafted the final logo, ensuring a seamless integration of design elements. The chosen blue (#219EBC) for the logo aligns with the primary accent color, creating a cohesive visual identity that resonates with the overall aesthetic of the brand.
Finalized Logo in Canva
LOW-FIDELITY PROTOTYPES
Next, I translated the conceptual sketches into a low-fidelity prototype through Figma, mainly focusing on key screens and functions.
HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPES
Advancing from the foundational low-fidelity prototypes, I elevated the design to a high-fidelity stage using Figma. This phase focused on refining the visual aesthetics, incorporating the finalized color palette, typography, and detailed user interface elements.
